Beginners guide to growing chillies!

What you need Getting Started Planting General Care

What you will need.

To begin with you will need some equipment which are all are cheap and easy to find in any garden store or on Amazon.

This includes:

  • A Propagator
  • Seeds
  • Zip lock bags
  • Potting soil
  • Biodegradable seedling pots
  • Plastic pots - 5 to 6inches

Getting Started.

You will first need to prepare your seeds, this is done by germination which is the process of a seed growing shoots and is simple to do.

  1. You will need to get 2 pieces of kitchen roll.
  2. Soak a piece lightly with water
  3. Arrange your seeds in a grid on the piece
  4. Place the second piece on top.
  5. Fold the roll up and place inside the zip lock bag and leave on a warm surface for a couple of days(3-4 days).

When you notice tails coming out from your seeds you are then ready to move onto the next step and plant them.

Planting.

Now that your seeds have grown tails you are ready to plant them!
You will now need to prepare your seedling pots and this is quite simple.
Fill the pot with as much soil as you can and then run some lukewarm water through the soil pressing down gently to compact the soil, and repeat until the pot is full.
Once your pots are ready, make a small hole about 1cm deep and place 1 seed per pot and cover with soil.
The prepared pots can now be placed in a propagator which can be placed on a window sill so that the plants can get sunlight to help them develop.

General Care.

As your chillies grow you will need to adjust how you care for them, here are a few tips in order to get the best out of your plant.

  • As your plant grows larger be sure to repot into a larger plastic pot.
  • Water your plants only when the top of the soil feels dry.
  • Fertilizer is a great option if you want your plants to grow large and strong.
  • When your plant starts flowering it is recommend to place it outside for insects to pollinate.

Chilli Varieties!

Carolina Reaper

shu:

1,641,183

Previously known as the hottest chilli in the world, now passed by the Dragon's Breath chilli and Pepper X.
The Reaper has a sweet and fruity flavour, before the intense heat kicks in.
Not for the faint of heart but definitely for people looking for a challenge.

Naga Viper

shu:

1,382,118

Another chilli that used to be the hottest in the world until 2017 when the Carolina Reaper was grown.
Described to have a sweet and fruity taste with a little tang, the chilli has a slower burn that allows the flavours to stay longer.

Habanero

shu:

350,000

The Habanero is a very popular chilli that you will often see it in stores being sold in the form of hot sauce.
A great all rounder chilli, suitable for use with any recipes with its tropical, fruity flavor and subtle smokiness to taste.

Jalapeño

shu:

3,600

A great beginner chilli to grow, popular around the world and fantastic as a nachos or chilli topper when pickled.
Very often Jalapeño's are roasted to provide a richer, slightly smoky, earthy taste with good heat.

Scotch Bonnet

shu:

350,000

A chilli similar to the Habanero that originates from the Caribbean.
Described as a slightly sweet taste to it, sort of like a tomato with a slight hint of apples and cherries.

Bird's Eye Chilli

shu:

100,000

Another popular chilli that can pack quite a punch but not too much to make you sweat.
With every chilli, you’ll taste hints of fruit-like flavors.
If you pick a bird’s eye while it’s still green, it will taste extra sour and citrusy.

Note: SHU stands for Scoville Heat Unit and is used as a way to measure how hot a chilli is.
